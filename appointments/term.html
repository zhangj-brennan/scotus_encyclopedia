Number of Justices Appointed by Presidential Term

In chronological order

Note:

Presidents with no nominations: William H. Harrison, Zachary Taylor, Andrew Johnson, Jimmy Carter
Recess appointments and elevations:
George Washington - Thomas Johnson was appointed in August 1791 and ultimatley confirmed in Nov 1791
John Adams- Bushrod Washington was appointed in Sep 1798 and ultimatley confirmed in Dec 1798; and Alfred Moore appointed Oct 1799 and confirmed Dec 1799
Thomas Jefferson- Henry B. Livingston was appointed in Nov 1806 and ultimatley confirmed in Dec 1806
James Monroe- Smith Thompson was appointed in Sept 1823 and ultimatley confirmed in Dec 1823
Martin Van Buren - John McKinley was appointed in April 1837 and ultimatley confirmed in Sept 1837
James K. Polk - Levi Woodbury was appointed in Sept 1845 and ultimatley confirmed in Jan 1846
Millard Fillmore - Benjamin Curtis was appointed in Sept 1851 and ultimatley confirmed in Dec 1851
Abraham Lincoln- David Davis was appointed in Oct 1862 and ultimatley confirmed in Dec 1862.
Theodore Roosevelt - Oliver Wendell Holmes was appointed in Aug 1902 and ultimatley confirmed in Dec 1902 [appointed by Roosevelt]
William Howard Taft - Associate Justice White elevation to Chief Justice
Herbert Hoover - (One of these nominations is Charles Evan Hughes who previously served on the Court)
Franklin D. Roosevelt - PLUS Associate Justice Stone elevation to Chief Justice
Dwight D. Eisenhower - Earl Warren was appointed in Oct 1953 and ultimatley confirmed in March 1954; William Brennan was appointed in Oct 1956 and ultimatley confirmed in March 1957; Potter Stewart was appointed in Oct 1958 and ultimatley confirmed in May 1959
Ronald Reagan - Associate Justice Rehnquist elevation to Chief Justice
